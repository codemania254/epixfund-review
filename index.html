EpixFund.com Review – Is EpixFund a Scam or Legit Broker?

EpixFund.com is a newly launched CFD broker that has begun attracting attention with bold claims and aggressive marketing. In this expert EpixFund review, we analyze whether epixfund.com is legit or if it is possibly a scam targeting inexperienced traders.

Basic Information About EpixFund

  • Website: epixfund.com
  • Domain Registration: April 2025 (very recent)
  • Claimed Location: London, United Kingdom
  • Trading Platform: MT4/MT5 (claimed)
  • Account Types: Bronze ($6,000), Silver ($15,000), Gold ($25,000), Platinum ($50,000), Diamond ($100,000), VIP ($200,000)

Is EpixFund.com Regulated?

No. Despite claiming to operate from the UK, EpixFund.com is not registered or licensed by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA). A search on the official FCA register returns no results for this broker.

They also falsely claim to be operated by Ovex (Pty) Ltd, allegedly licensed by South Africa’s FSCA. We verified this claim through the FSCA database and found no such license associated with the entity. This is clear evidence of deception.

Account Types – High Minimum Deposits with No Demo

EpixFund offers six types of trading accounts, starting from a shocking minimum deposit of $6,000 for the Bronze account. This is an extremely high barrier to entry, especially considering the platform does not offer a free demo account. Here’s the breakdown:

  • Bronze: $6,000
  • Silver: $15,000
  • Gold: $25,000
  • Platinum: $50,000
  • Diamond: $100,000
  • VIP: $200,000

With no track record and no license, entrusting such high amounts to this broker is highly risky.

EpixFund as a Prop Trading Firm – A Conflict of Interest?

Interestingly, EpixFund markets itself as a prop trading firm as well. Traders must pass a two-step evaluation and, if successful, are promised 60–90% profit splits. This model is typical among unlicensed “prop firms” offering fake funding.

The real concern is conflict of interest: A broker cannot act both as the market maker and the funding body. They control the rules, the demo accounts, and the outcome — all while collecting non-refundable fees from traders who often fail arbitrary challenges. This setup benefits only the firm, not the traders.

Marketing Claims and Transparency Issues

EpixFund’s website is filled with unverifiable and misleading claims. They mention being featured in financial publications and quote vague success statistics — yet provide no third-party validation or independent reviews.

Additionally, while they promote offering MT4/MT5 platforms and label themselves as an “STP broker,” their structure, lack of regulation, and behavior align more closely with a market maker model — profiting when clients lose trades.

Final Verdict – Is EpixFund.com a Scam?

EpixFund.com raises multiple red flags that should concern any trader:

  • No regulatory license (FCA or FSCA)
  • Recent domain registration (April 2025)
  • Exorbitant minimum deposits
  • No demo account or transparency
  • Misleading claims about regulation and publicity
  • Operating as a supposed prop firm — a serious conflict of interest

Until proven otherwise, we consider EpixFund to be a high-risk and potentially fraudulent broker. Traders should avoid depositing funds with this entity.
